### Settlement-rule traps

- No-confirmation tail: Bondi already departed, Blanche serves as Acting AG indefinitely under FVRA + DAG-succession, no permanent nominee ever clears the Senate before Jan 20 2029 → market resolves NO to every named candidate. This 'long acting' branch is materially nonzero and gets zero direct ticker on Kalshi; it has to be inferred from the residual.
- Recess appointment ambiguity: rules say recess appointments count 'even if struck down.' But what about an intra-session adjournment-clause appointment that the Senate never recognizes as a recess (NLRB v. Noel Canning territory)? Could resolve YES on a technicality that markets are not modeling, or NO if Kalshi interprets 'recess' strictly.
- Tie-vote / VP-breaking: a 50-50 confirmation with VP Vance breaking the tie counts as a normal confirmation, but if a senator is absent / hospitalized / dies, a 49-49 or 50-49 vote could leave the nominee in limbo for weeks. Settlement timing matters if competing nominations are stacked.
- Withdrawn-then-resubmitted same person: if Blanche is nominated, withdrawn, then re-nominated and confirmed, he is still the 'first NEW' AG. Markets sometimes mis-handle the identity-vs-process distinction.
- Definition of 'NEW': Blanche is currently Acting AG. If Trump uses a recess appointment to install Blanche himself, does Kalshi count that as the 'first NEW AG' or treat Blanche as not-new because he was already running DOJ? Rule text says recess appointments are included, which implies YES on Blanche-via-recess, but the 'NEW person' phrasing is exploitable.
- Cross-market contamination: if there are separate Kalshi tickers for 'Blanche confirmed by [date]' vs 'next AG is Blanche,' arbitrage flow can distort this market's prints without reflecting fundamentals.

### Narrative traps

- Trump-allies-on-X effect: loud public endorsements of Blanche (JonHollis-type accounts, MAGA influencers) get scraped by prediction-market traders as 'signal' when they are actually a coordinated lobbying campaign aimed at Trump himself — i.e., the noise IS the strategy.
- Recency illusion: Blanche is running DOJ today, so traders mentally collapse 'current AG' into 'next confirmed AG.' The market is pricing presence, not Senate math.
- Media speculation laundering: cable hits and Politico/Axios 'sources say' pieces recycle the same 2-3 names (Blanche, Zeldin, maybe a sitting senator), creating an illusion of a narrow field when the true distribution includes dark-horse state AGs, federal judges, and sitting US Attorneys.
- Gaetz-amnesia: traders who lived through the Gaetz withdrawal in late 2024 are now under-weighting the same dynamic for Blanche despite a near-identical conflict profile.
- Survivorship bias in the X timeline: posts predicting Blanche's confirmation get amplified; posts noting Tillis/Collins skepticism get buried. Sentiment-scraping bots feed this asymmetry back into the order book.
- False binary framing: discourse treats it as 'Blanche vs Zeldin' when the rule-relevant outcome space includes (a) neither — long acting tenure to 2029, (b) a surprise pick, (c) recess-appointment gambit that may or may not settle YES depending on Kalshi adjudication.
