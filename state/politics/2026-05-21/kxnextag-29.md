## 2. Settlement Rules

### What counts

```
Per Kalshi KXNEXTAG rules_primary: the market resolves YES for candidate X if X is the first NEW person to be Attorney General before Jan 20, 2029 15:00Z. Resolution covers the next confirmed AG after the current vacancy/acting period.
```

### Acting / Interim treatment

```
Verbatim from KXNEXTAG rules_secondary: 'Acting and Interim holders of the office are not included in the Payout Criterion. Persons confirmed by the Senate to be that position, or appointed via a "recess appointment" are included, even if that recess appointment is later struck down as illegal.'
```

### Ambiguities

- 'First new person' is undefined relative to the prior incumbent — unclear if re-nomination/re-confirmation of the same person would count as 'new'.
- Recess appointments count even if later struck down as illegal, but rules don't specify trigger timing (immediately on appointment, or only after some period).
- No explicit handling of a nominee who is confirmed but never sworn in, dies, or withdraws before assuming office.
- Deadline is Jan 20, 2029 15:00Z; ambiguous what happens if a confirmation vote occurs but swearing-in slips past that timestamp.
- Only listed candidates have YES markets — if a person not on the candidate roster becomes AG, none of the YES contracts resolve YES.

## 8. Skeptic Review

### Strongest reason the favorite may be wrong

```
Blanche is already serving as Acting AG with no permanent nominee submitted 7 weeks after Bondi's firing — if Trump wanted him in the chair he would have been nominated already. The delay signals the WH is shopping alternatives, and Blanche's recusal/independence baggage plus the Tillis Jan-6 litmus test make Senate math harder than 44¢ implies.
```

### Strongest reason a non-favorite may be underpriced

```
Harmeet Dhillon at an implied low-single-digit price (publicly floated, MAGA-aligned, RNC legal background, no EPA-style double-confirm burden like Zeldin and fewer on-camera landmines than Pirro) is the cleanest compromise pick if Blanche stalls — her tail is materially thicker than a 22-way uniform would suggest.
```

### Settlement-rule traps

- Recess appointment that is later struck down by courts still resolves YES — traders pricing only Senate-confirmation paths underweight this.
- Nominee confirmed by Senate but not sworn in before Jan 20, 2029 has undefined treatment.
- Nominee dies or withdraws after confirmation vote but before taking office — undefined.
- FVRA 210-day expiry (~late Oct 2026) could force a non-Blanche nominee suddenly, but market may not reprice until a name leaks.
- Blanche being formally nominated does NOT resolve the market — only confirmation/recess appointment does, so a nomination bump is a fade trap.
- 'Acting' Blanche continuing past 210 days via statutory workaround does not count as resolution.

### Narrative traps

- X rumor-tier chatter for Blanche creating false confirmation bias — no hard reporting cited.
- 'Loyalty pick = inevitable' heuristic ignores Trump's pattern of late-stage surprise nominees.
- Zeldin price rising read as signal, but he carries a double-confirm burden — the move may be noise.
- Pirro's MAGA-base narrative obscures concrete Collins/Murkowski vote-count problem.
- Anchoring on the 54¢→44¢ Blanche drop as 'oversold' rather than as information that insiders see the nomination slipping.
- Assuming the 22-way book's tail names are uniformly dead — FVRA pressure plus Tillis litmus could elevate a dark horse not currently on the floated list.
