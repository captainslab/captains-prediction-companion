### 3A. MONEYLINE
**Kalshi price:** Allen ~59¢ / Costa ~41¢ (fight-day morning, BettorsInsider Kalshi cite)
**Sportsbook ref:** Allen -145 to -154 / Costa +120 to +125

**Research basis:**

Allen advantages:
- Technical precision vs. Costa's explosive-but-raw style
- Southpaw mirror-stance dynamic: both southpaws — traditional dominant outside angle for orthodox vs. southpaw goes away; becomes a range management fight where Allen's experience matters
- 72% TD defense vs. Costa's 40% TD accuracy — Allen should be able to stuff takedowns
- 8 of last 10 fights went to decision — he makes fights go long, which is where Costa fades
- Durability: never stopped; can absorb Costa's early burst without crisis
- Tristar camp (Firas Zahabi) specifically prepares for 5-round championship-format chess

Costa advantages:
- 6-fight win streak (all FW), 7-0 at 145
- Size: 5'10"/71" vs. Allen's 5'8"/70" — reach and height in southpaw mirror means Costa's jab lands first
- Higher volume: 4.38 vs. 3.47 sig/min — if Costa sets the pace, output gap compounds
- R1 threat: 11 career R1 finishes; spinning back kick finish vs. Ige shows he can end fights with a single strike
- Allen's recent form: 1-3 in last 4 fights (all decision losses)

**Inference:** Market correctly identifies Allen as moderate favorite. The case for Allen is predicated on experience, technical edge, and Costa's untested 5-round cardio. Costa's path to victory runs through R1-R2 finish before Allen's conditioning advantage compounds. Allen fades if he can't control pace or if his passive stretches continue.

**Label:** LEAN (Allen) — supported by technical + experience edge and Costa's cardio question over 5 rounds, but Costa's finish threat in early rounds is genuine
