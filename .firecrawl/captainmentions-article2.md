> SECTION A — What to expect. Environment and situation
> Trump is speaking at a Small Business Summit, not a foreign-policy briefing, war-room presser, or national security event.
> That matters.
> Reality:
> This board should price small-business language first: taxes, economy, investment, American workers, opportunity, and Trump’s domestic-brand phrases.
> Record:
> Social recency can create heat, but heat is not settlement. A word can be everywhere online and still never be spoken in this exact event.
> Settlement:
> The word needs to be stated by Trump during the event and documented by an eligible source. Paraphrase, summaries, social posts, analyst discussion, and vibe do not settle the market.
> Tree-in-the-Forest Problem:
> If Trump says the word but no eligible outlet quotes or transcribes it, does the market hear it?
> The major board trap is Iran. It is hot socially, but the event setup does not naturally force it. This is a small-business room. Pricing Iran like a lock confuses topic heat with event fit.

> SECTION F — Sneaky NOs
> New York — Trump can talk about business, taxes, and the economy without needing a New York reference. No obvious event hook.
> Hottest — the meaning can show up through “strongest,” “best,” “greatest,” or “booming” without the exact resolving word.
> Trillion — bill size and budget talk can be framed as “massive,” “historic,” “largest,” or “hundreds of billions” without saying the exact word.
> SECTION G — Quick checklist. Counts / doesn’t count
> Counts:
> Trump saying the listed word or phrase during the summit.
> A qualifying outlet documenting the word in a quote or transcript.
> Direct event remarks, prepared or ad-libbed.
> Doesn’t count:
> Trump posting the word on social media.
> Reporters, analysts, or anchors discussing the word.
> Articles summarizing the topic without quoting Trump.
> Similar words, substitutes, or general topic references.
> Settlement is proof-first. Trade the transcript path, not the social feed.
